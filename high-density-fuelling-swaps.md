# High-Density Fuelling Swaps — Plug & Play

Built around food you already eat. The goal: add ~800 cal and ~45g protein **without adding bulk** — because you're full, not unwilling.

Your day today landed at roughly **1,600 cal / 135g protein / 170g carbs / 42g fat** vs a target of ~2,400 cal / 180g protein. Every swap below is designed to close that gap invisibly.

---

## The single highest-leverage change

**Make every protein shake with whole milk, not water.**

- Pro Milk Salted Caramel + 250ml whole milk instead of water → **+160 cal, +8g protein**
- Do it twice a day = +320 cal, +16g protein, zero extra fullness.

That one habit alone closes a third of your gap.

---

## Breakfast swaps (you ate 355 cal — push to ~600)

**Keep:** Protein Salted Caramel pot + Pro Milk shake.

**Add one of these (no new shopping needed):**

| Add-on | Calories | Protein | Why it works |
|---|---|---|---|
| 40g oats stirred into the shake/yoghurt | +150 | +5g | Slow carbs, barely fills you |
| 1 tbsp peanut butter | +100 | +4g | Pure dense fat, melts into anything |
| Handful (30g) nuts on the side | +180 | +6g | Eat while doing something else |
| 1 banana | +105 | +1g | Easy carbs for morning training |

**Target combo:** shake-with-milk + oats + banana = a 600+ cal breakfast you'll barely notice.

---

## Lunch swaps (684 cal — already your best meal)

This one's fine. Two tiny upgrades:

- **Drizzle 1 tbsp olive oil or sesame oil** over the egg fried rice → +120 cal, no fullness.
- **Double the Getpro yoghurt** (it's only 86 cal / 15g protein) → +86 cal, +15g protein. Cheapest protein win on your whole list.

---

## Dinner swaps (404 cal — your biggest hole, push to ~700)

The basa-rice-veg plate is lean to a fault. Fixes:

| Change | Calories | Protein |
|---|---|---|
| **Double the basa** (2 × 100g not 1) | +124 | +20g |
| 1 tbsp olive/sesame oil on the rice or veg | +120 | — |
| Swap to a fattier fish occasionally (salmon vs basa) | +150 | +5g |
| Add 100g prawns to the stir-fry | +85 | +18g |

**Target combo:** double basa + oil + a few prawns = ~700 cal, ~55g protein dinner.

---

## The "I'm full but under-fuelled" rescue: drink it

When you genuinely can't eat more, **liquid calories bypass fullness.** Build this once a day:

**Recovery smoothie (≈500 cal, 35g protein):**
- 250ml whole milk
- 1 scoop / pot of your salted caramel protein
- 1 banana
- 40g oats
- 1 tbsp peanut butter

Blend, drink post-ride. This single smoothie is bigger than your entire dinner was today, and it goes down when solid food won't.

---

## Around training (matters more than daily totals)

- **Within 60 min of finishing a ride:** carbs + protein. The smoothie above is ideal, or shake + banana.
- Don't let the post-session appetite dip trick you into skipping — that's exactly when the hole gets dug.

---

## Daily target checklist

- [ ] Both shakes made with **whole milk**
- [ ] **Oats** added somewhere (breakfast or smoothie)
- [ ] **Oil** on at least one savoury meal
- [ ] **Double protein** at dinner (fish/prawns) OR a second yoghurt at lunch
- [ ] One **smoothie** on training days

Hit 4 of 5 and you're at target without ever feeling stuffed.
