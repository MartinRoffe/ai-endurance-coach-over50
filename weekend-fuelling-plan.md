# Weekend Fuelling Plan — Solo Long Rides (2–6 hours)

Built around the 10 recipes: 4 overnight oats, 2 sweet rice cakes, 2 savoury rice cakes, 1 flapjack, 1 energy ball batch. Full methods in the dashboard at **Nutrition → Recipes → Weekend Ride Fuel** (`/nutrition/recipes/weekend-fuel`).

**Summer model:** all ride carbs from **solids** (rice cakes, flapjack, energy balls). Bottles are **electrolyte-heavy only** — fluid + sodium, not maltodextrin carb drinks. See **Nutrition → Ride Fuelling** for the full protocol.

> **Saturday rucks** use the post-ruck porridge + eggs + yogurt from Daily Menus — not this oats rotation.

## The framework (use this every Saturday and Sunday)

Early start means there's no 2–3 h window to digest a full breakfast beforehand. The plan flips: a small, light bite right before rolling out, fuel properly on the bike from early on (solids + electrolyte bottles), and the overnight oats jar — made the night before with whey — becomes **recovery** once you're back.

| Ride length | Just before rolling out (10–15 min) | On the bike (solids) | Hydration (electrolyte bottles) | Recovery |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **2–3 h** | Half a banana, or one rice cake square | 1–2 pieces total (~45–60 g/hr if pushing; 1 piece by 30 min, optional 2nd by 90 min on easy spin) | **500 ml/hr** — 1 bottle pre-mixed (E1); sip from minute 0 | Tier 1: chocolate milk if >2 h · Tier 2: 1 oats jar **≥30 g protein** |
| **3–4.5 h** | Half a banana or small flapjack piece | **60–70 g/hr** — **~2 pieces/hour** (e.g. 1 rice cake + 2 balls ≈ 57 g) from min 15–20; 25–30 min timer | **500–750 ml/hr** — E1 + E2; ¼ tsp salt + pinch lite salt per 500 ml | Tier 1: chocolate milk in car · Tier 2: full oats jar **≥30 g protein** within 60 min |
| **4.5–6 h** | Half a banana or flapjack + coffee if habit | **70–90 g/hr** — **2–3 pieces/hour**; savoury rice cakes hours 1–2, sweet cakes + energy balls after hour 3; flapjack as bonk insurance | **600–800 ml/hr** in heat — 2 electrolyte bottles; refill at café; optional 200 ml coconut water per bottle | Tier 1: chocolate milk before shower · Tier 2: full oats jar **35–40 g protein** |

Starting to fuel earlier on the bike than you normally would matters more now — you're rolling out closer to empty than off a full breakfast, so don't wait an hour before the first bite.

### Summer electrolyte bottle (per 500 ml)

All ride hydration — **no carb powder in the bottles**.

- **500 ml** water (or 300 ml water + **200 ml coconut water**)
- **¼ tsp** table salt (~575 mg sodium)
- **Pinch lite salt** (potassium)
- **1 tsp** honey or sugar (helps sodium uptake; ~5 g carbs — ignore for fuel maths)
- Juice of **½ lemon**
- Shake hard. One bottle ≈ one hour of fluid in cool weather; two bottles on 3 h+ rides.

**Friday prep:** pre-mix and label **E1**, **E2** (and E3/E4 for 5–6 h days). Fridge overnight or cool bag for morning.

### Rough fuel reference

**Solids (count toward hourly carb target):**
- Sweet or savoury rice cake square ≈ **25–30 g** carbs
- Flapjack bar ≈ **30–35 g** carbs (most energy-dense — bonk insurance)
- Energy ball ≈ **15 g** carbs (eat **two together** for ~30 g)
- Half banana ≈ **12 g** carbs

**Recovery overnight oats jar:**
- **50–70 g** carbs depending on recipe
- **≥30 g protein** every variant (1 scoop whey + 100 g Greek yogurt baseline; see Recipes)

**Recovery stack (rides >2 h):**
1. **Within 20 min:** recovery chocolate milk (~30 g C / ~10 g P) — drink before shower; pre-cool in the car
2. **Within 45–60 min:** full protein overnight oats jar

One practical note: on 4–6 h days, don't use the ride as a place to bank a calorie deficit. Under-fuelling a long solo ride just means you bonk, then overeat in the evening to compensate — worse for the fat-loss goal than eating properly on the bike. Save the deficit for rest days.

**Summer appetite:** savoury tolerance drops in heat as well as on long rides — front-load bacon/avocado rice cakes in hours 1–2, switch to cinnamon/PB&J cakes and date balls when heavy food repels.

## Two-weekend rotation (so nothing gets boring)

Rice cakes and flapjack keep for days — batch every other week and eat through it.

### Weekend A

**Saturday (the bigger day)**
- Just before: half a banana
- On the bike: Bacon & Egg Rice Cakes (savoury, first half) → Flapjack bar hour 2–3 → Energy balls closing hour · **E1 + E2**
- Recovery: chocolate milk → **EF Pro Cycling Athlete Overnight Oats**

**Sunday (recovery-paced spin)**
- Just before: one rice cake square
- On the bike: Avocado & Egg Rice Cakes → Team Sky Cinnamon Rice Cakes if it runs long · **E1**
- Recovery: chocolate milk → **Banoffee Overnight Oats**

### Weekend B

**Saturday**
- Just before: half a banana
- On the bike: PB&J Sweet Rice Cakes → Bacon & Egg Rice Cakes mid-ride → Flapjack last push · **E1 + E2**
- Recovery: chocolate milk → **Strawberries 'n' Cream Overnight Oats**

**Sunday**
- Just before: small piece of flapjack
- On the bike: Team Sky Cinnamon Rice Cakes → Energy balls · **E1**
- Recovery: chocolate milk → **Cycling Labs Classic Overnight Oats**

Repeat A/B fortnightly, or mix and match once you know which combos sit best at hour 4 vs hour 1.

## Batch-cook schedule & right-sizing portions

Make the **recovery oats jar the night before each ride** — stir whey in at prep time so every jar hits ≥30 g protein.

The 24-hour fridge-set time for rice cakes is fixed regardless of batch size — making less saves ingredients, not time. Worth doing where shelf life is short and the full recipe yields more than you'll get through solo.

| Item | Full batch yields | Shelf life | Smaller batch worth making? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Overnight oats (any) | 1 jar | 3–5 days fridge | Single-serving — one jar the night before each ride |
| PB&J Rice Cakes | 6 pieces | 4–5 days fridge, don't freeze | Optional — half batch (3 pieces): 90 g rice, 180 ml water, ½ tbsp sugar, ¼ tbsp salt, 1 tbsp peanut butter, 1 tbsp jam |
| Team Sky Cinnamon Rice Cakes | 20 pieces | 4–5 days fridge | **Yes** — quarter batch (~5 pieces): 125 g rice, 200 ml water, 1 tbsp coconut oil, ½ tsp cinnamon, 2¼ tsp coconut sugar, 75 g cream cheese, 25 ml agave |
| Avocado & Egg Rice Cakes | 6 pieces | 3–4 days fridge (egg/avocado) | Yes — half batch (3 pieces): 90 g rice, 180 ml water, ½ tbsp sugar, ¼ tbsp salt, ¼ avocado, 1 egg, 15 g nuts |
| Bacon & Egg Rice Cakes | 10 pieces | 3–4 days fridge (egg/bacon) | Yes, unless riding 4+ times that week — half batch (5 pieces): 200 g rice, 350 ml water, 4 oz bacon, 2 eggs, 1 tbsp liquid aminos, 1 tbsp brown sugar, ¼ tsp salt, 1 tbsp parmesan |
| Flapjack | 12 bars | 1–2 weeks airtight, freezes well | Not necessary — long shelf life covers the full batch |
| Energy balls | 10 balls (~35 g each) | 10 days fridge / 3 weeks frozen | Not necessary — same reason |

**Friday / Saturday eve prep checklist:** wrap rice cakes in foil · mix **E1/E2** electrolyte bottles · pack flapjack + balls in jersey · pre-cool chocolate milk · build tomorrow's oats jar with whey.
