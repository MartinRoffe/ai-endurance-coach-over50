Masters athlete

WHY OVER-50 IS DIFFERENT

Your fuelling strategy can't mirror a 30-year-old's. These five shifts explain why the numbers in this guide are calibrated the way they are.

1

Glycogen kinetics slow. Storage capacity drops slightly and post-exercise resynthesis is slower. You can't under-fuel long rides and expect the same recovery trajectory — the Sunday ride must be fuelled even in a deficit phase.

2

Protein needs a higher leucine threshold. After 50, meaningful muscle protein synthesis needs ~3 g leucine — roughly 30–40 g protein in the post-ride window. A token snack isn't enough.

3

Fat oxidation is a strength. Older athletes often oxidise fat well in Zone 1–2. Leverage it on easy sessions — but never at the expense of hard efforts or rides beyond 90 minutes.

4

Gut function needs longer to adapt. Blood flow to the gut can drop ~80% during intense exercise. Gut training to 90 g/hr takes consistent weeks of practice — start early, not on event day.

5

Morning cortisol is already elevated. Early fasted starts without carbs spike cortisol further and promote muscle catabolism. Even 15–20 min pre-ride carbs blunt this — the bedside bottle matters.

Scenario A

WEEKEND LONG RIDES

You cannot rely on a proper pre-ride breakfast. The fix: night-before loading + a small pre-roll bite + solids on the bike from minute one + electrolyte bottles for fluid and salt. Build tomorrow's protein overnight oats jar the night before — that's recovery, not breakfast.

Recipe: one standard rice cake batch — pick any recovery oats flavour post-ride. No rotation schedule to track.

Night before

FRIor SAT

Carb-forward dinner

Carb-rich griddle night (Saturday) or paella-style rice — generous rice, easy on fat and fibre. Target 2–3 g carbs/kg across dinner + a small evening snack (rice pudding, malt loaf, yogurt + honey).

  • Avoid heavy alcohol, very spicy food, or a huge late meal
  • Optional: sip 200–300 ml electrolyte mix with dinner if appetite is low

Wake-up (5–20 min before out the door)

A

Grab-and-go solids (preferred)

Half a banana (~12 g), one rice cake square (~27 g), or a small flapjack piece + black coffee. Optional: sip 200 ml from pre-mixed electrolyte bottle E1.

B

Medjool dates (fast carbs)

2 Medjool dates (~36 g) + water or electrolyte — good when you have literally 5 minutes.

Avoid on waking: eggs, bacon, full porridge bowl, coffee-only, high-fibre muesli, or a full overnight oats jar — save the oats for post-ride recovery (≥30 g protein). Target 15–40 g fast carbs + 300–500 ml fluid before/at start — the rest comes from pockets on the bike.

Scenario B

EVENING RIDES

Most weekday sessions are 45–90 min. Lunch 3–4 h earlier is the real pre-ride meal — not what you eat at 5 pm.

Lunch (the hidden pre-ride meal)

Aim for ~1.5–2 g carbs/kg at lunch on ride days: batch rice + chicken, pasta + tuna, or a substantial sandwich + fruit. Moderate protein, not a huge fat load. Hydrate through the afternoon — 500 ml+ before kitting up.

Recovery window

IMMEDIATE POST-RIDE

The 45-minute window after long rides is when glycogen repletion and muscle repair are most efficient. Over-50 athletes benefit from being more deliberate here, not less — you need 30–40 g protein to hit the leucine threshold that triggers meaningful muscle protein synthesis.

Tier 1 · within 20 min

Always

Recovery chocolate milk — 300 ml semi-skimmed + cocoa + tsp honey. Drink before showering. Pre-cool a bottle in the car if driving home.

~30 g C · ~10 g P
Tier 2 · within 45–60 min

Long rides >2 h

Protein overnight oats jar — full jar, ≥30 g protein (whey + Greek yogurt). Alternate: recovery plate with ~1 g carbs/kg + 30–40 g protein (big porridge, rice + chicken).

50–70 g C · 30–40 g P
Tier 3 · next 2–3 h

Keep eating

Normal meals. Don't "save calories" after a 4–6 h ride. Spread protein (30–40 g/meal). Avoid heavy alcohol same afternoon.

Protein spread · sleep

Tier 4 · post-ride meal (60–90 min): consolidate recovery with 40–50 g protein, carbs scaled to ride duration (80–120 g after 4–6 h), vegetables for micronutrients. Over-50 priority add-ons: turmeric + black pepper (anti-inflammatory), tart cherry juice (200 ml — muscle soreness), collagen (10 g in warm water — connective tissue), magnesium-rich foods (leafy greens, pumpkin seeds — also aids sleep).

Over-50 reminders

→

Start at 60 g/hr and add 10–15 g/hr every 2–3 weeks on long rides rather than jumping straight to 90 g.

→

Under-fuelling long rides → poor sleep → lower HRV next day. Your dashboard will show it.

→

In a deficit phase, cut carbs on rest days and easy evenings, not on Sunday 4 h Z2.

→

Daily protein (~1.6–2.0 g/kg) matters more than one heroic post-ride steak.

Common mistakes

✕ Waiting until hour 2 to eat on fasted early starts
✕ Carb maltodextrin bottles on summer weekend long rides — use solids + electrolyte instead
✕ Eating the full overnight oats jar before the ride — it's post-ride recovery food
✕ New recipes or shop-bought gels on event day only
✕ Skipping post-ride because "dinner is soon" after 3 h+
✕ Over-eating before evening rides when lunch was adequate
✕ Forgetting salt in homemade drinks — ¼ tsp per 500 ml is not optional
✕ High-fat food on high-intensity efforts — nuts, cheese and fatty foods slow gastric emptying above Zone 3
✕ Under-fuelling for body composition — chronic ride under-fuelling drives muscle catabolism, not fat loss
