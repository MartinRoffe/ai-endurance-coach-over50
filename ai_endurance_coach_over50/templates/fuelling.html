Your schedule

TWO DIFFERENT PROBLEMS

Weekend long rides (2–6 h) and weekday evening rides need opposite pre-ride strategies. Get this wrong and you'll bonk on Sunday or feel heavy on Tuesday.

Scenario A · Sat & Sun

Long rides · 2–6 hours

Start time
Very early — quiet roads, little time to eat
Pre-ride challenge
Can't do a proper breakfast. Saturday's carb-rich griddle dinner is the real pre-fuel.
During priority
High carb rate from minute 0. Train toward 80–90 g/hr on the longest rides.
Post priority
Aggressive recovery window, then a proper meal. Chocolate milk before shower.
Scenario B · Tue–Fri evenings

Shorter rides · 45–90 min

Start time
Post-lunch, pre-dinner — typically 3–5 h after lunch
Pre-ride challenge
Lunch is your main fuel. Light top-up only if needed — don't over-eat.
During priority
Water only under 60 min. One carb bottle or banana for 75–90 min sessions.
Post priority
Recovery bridge to dinner — chocolate milk, not a second dinner.

Gut training: weeks 5–8 practise 60 g/hr on every ride ≥75 min. Weeks 9–11 push to 75–90 g/hr on long rides. Use your exact homemade mix now — nothing new on event day. The deficit belongs to rest days, not the key ride.

Scenario A

WEEKEND LONG RIDES

You cannot rely on a proper pre-ride breakfast. The fix: night-before loading + wake-up liquid carbs + aggressive on-bike fuelling from minute one.

Night before

FRIor SAT

Carb-forward dinner

Carb-rich griddle night (Saturday) or paella-style rice — generous rice, easy on fat and fibre. Target 2–3 g carbs/kg across dinner + a small evening snack (rice pudding, malt loaf, yogurt + honey).

  • Avoid heavy alcohol, very spicy food, or a huge late meal
  • Optional: sip 200–300 ml standard drink mix with dinner if appetite is low

Wake-up (5–20 min before out the door)

A

Bedside bottle — best for 5:30 am rollouts

Pre-mix 300–400 ml standard drink (24–48 g carbs) the night before. Drink half on waking, half in the first 15 min on the bike.

B

Grab-and-go solids (10–15 min)

1 sweet rice cake (~35 g) or 2 date balls (~36 g) + 300 ml water with pinch of salt. Optional: half a banana.

C

Overnight oats jar (30 min before)

Small jar: oats + milk + honey + banana, made the night before. Eat half only — you don't want a full stomach at hour one.

Avoid on waking: eggs, bacon, full porridge bowl, coffee-only, high-fibre muesli. Target 40–80 g fast carbs + 500 ml fluid before/at start — the rest comes on the bike.

Scenario B

EVENING RIDES

Most weekday sessions are 45–90 min. Lunch 3–4 h earlier is the real pre-ride meal — not what you eat at 5 pm.

Lunch (the hidden pre-ride meal)

Aim for ~1.5–2 g carbs/kg at lunch on ride days: batch rice + chicken, pasta + tuna, or a substantial sandwich + fruit. Moderate protein, not a huge fat load. Hydrate through the afternoon — 500 ml+ before kitting up.

TimingWhat to do

≤60 min easyWater only — no food needed
60–75 min Z2Water; optional half banana at 45 min if hungry
75–90 min tempo1 standard bottle (60 g over the ride) OR bottle + half rice cake at 45 min

Post-ride bridge (within 20 min): homemade chocolate milk (300 ml) ≈ 30 g carbs + 10 g protein. Then normal dinner with extra rice/potato if it was a hard 90 min session. Don't starve until dinner — and don't eat so much recovery food that dinner becomes redundant.

Make at home

DRINKS & SOLID FUEL

Full step-by-step recipes live in the Recipes guide. These are your reference formulations — make them now, train on them every long ride.

Standard energy drink

~60 g carbs · per 500 ml · 1 bottle/hr
  • 40 g maltodextrin
  • 20 g fructose
  • ¼ tsp salt (~575 mg sodium)
  • Squash + 500 ml water

High-carb dual source

~90 g carbs · per 500 ml · long rides
  • 55 g maltodextrin
  • 35 g fructose
  • ¼ tsp salt + squash + lemon
  • 500 ml water — shake well, denser mix

Electrolyte only

Hydration · heat days · second bottle
  • 500 ml water + ¼ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar or honey
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Pinch lite salt (potassium) optional

Savoury rice cake

~30–40 g carbs · hours 2–5
  • 250 g sushi rice + eggs + bacon + soy
  • Press, cut into 8, foil-wrap
  • Real food when gels repel

Homemade gel flask

~25–30 g · per swig
  • 100 g maltodextrin + 3 tbsp honey
  • ~80 ml warm water + pinch salt + lemon
  • Chase with water to absorb

Solid carb reference

Count toward hourly target
  • Banana · ~25 g
  • Homemade oat bar · ~28 g
  • Date & oat ball · ~18 g (×2 ≈ gel)
  • Sweet rice cake · ~35 g

Recovery window

IMMEDIATE POST-RIDE

The 45-minute window after long rides is when glycogen repletion and muscle repair are most efficient. Over-50 athletes benefit from being more deliberate here, not less.

Tier 1 · within 20 min

Always

Recovery chocolate milk — 300 ml semi-skimmed + cocoa + tsp honey. Drink before showering. Pre-cool a bottle in the car if driving home.

~30 g C · ~10 g P
Tier 2 · within 45–60 min

Long rides >2 h

Recovery plate: ~1 g carbs/kg + 25–40 g protein. Big recovery porridge, or rice + 150 g chicken, or Sunday stir fry with generous rice.

60–120 g C · 25–40 g P
Tier 3 · next 2–3 h

Keep eating

Normal meals. Don't "save calories" after a 4–6 h ride. Spread protein (30–40 g/meal). Avoid heavy alcohol same afternoon.

Friday / Saturday evening

PREP CHECKLIST

15 minutes Friday or Saturday evening saves a fumbled 5:30 am start. Label bottles so you're not thinking on the bike.

1

Mix drink powders into labelled bottles

Tape: "H1 standard", "H2 high-carb", etc. Fridge or cool bag for morning.

2

Wrap rice cakes in foil

Pack jersey pockets the night before. Make 8–16 on Friday if Sunday is the big one.

3

Fill gel flask · set bedside wake-up bottle

300–400 ml standard mix ready to drink on waking.

4

Pre-cool recovery chocolate milk

In the fridge or an insulated bottle in the car for post-ride.

Over-50 reminders

→

Start at 60 g/hr and add 10–15 g/hr every 2–3 weeks on long rides rather than jumping straight to 90 g.

→

Under-fuelling long rides → poor sleep → lower HRV next day. Your dashboard will show it.

→

In a deficit phase, cut carbs on rest days and easy evenings, not on Sunday 4 h Z2.

→

Daily protein (~1.6–2.0 g/kg) matters more than one heroic post-ride steak.

Common mistakes

✕ Waiting until hour 2 to eat on fasted early starts
✕ All liquid, no solids on 4 h+ rides
✕ New recipes or shop-bought gels on event day only
✕ Skipping post-ride because "dinner is soon" after 3 h+
✕ Over-eating before evening rides when lunch was adequate
✕ Forgetting salt in homemade drinks — ¼ tsp per 500 ml is not optional
