14bWeekend Ride Fuel

Summer model: all ride carbs from these solids; bottles are electrolyte-only (see Ride Fuelling). Build one recovery oats jar the night before each ride — every variant below hits ≥30 g protein via whey + Greek yogurt. A/B rotation sequences are in the fuelling guide.

Recovery overnight oats (post-ride · 1 jar)

Shared base per 350 ml jar (same as weekday #05 — EF Pro, Strawberries, Classic): 50 g oats · 120 ml milk · 90 g Greek yogurt · 1 scoop whey (~30 g powder) · 1 tbsp chia · 1 tsp honey · pinch salt. Berries and fruit toppings in a separate pot. Mix, seal, fridge overnight. Eat the full jar within 45–60 min of finishing (after chocolate milk). ~50–65 g carbs · ~42 g protein. Banoffee is a standalone GCN recipe (~29 g protein from yogurt — see below).

Banoffee Overnight Oats
1 jar~29g P5 min prepGCN recipe
Ingredients
  • 90 g quick oats (about 1 cup)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Few drops of vanilla essence
  • 30 g muscovado sugar (2 tbsp — 1 tbsp in oats, 1 tbsp in yogurt)
  • 1 espresso (~50 ml)
  • 200 ml skimmed milk
  • 1 medium ripe banana (~100 g peeled), sliced
  • 150 g natural low-fat Greek-style yogurt
  • 10 g dark chocolate, grated (1 square — optional)
Method
  1. Add oats, salt, espresso, vanilla and 1 tbsp muscovado to a jar or tall glass; stir in the milk.
  2. Layer sliced banana on top — do not stir (you want distinct banoffee layers).
  3. Mix the remaining 1 tbsp muscovado into the yogurt; pour over the banana — again, don't stir.
  4. Seal and fridge overnight. Oats soak up the liquid and go creamy; yogurt sets as a toffee topping.
  5. Grate dark chocolate on top before eating if using. Eat the full jar within 45–60 min of finishing (after chocolate milk).
Standalone recipe — no shared base or whey scoop; ~29 g protein from yogurt (Emma's GCN Banoffee Pie Overnight Oats). ~760 kcal · ~132 g carbs. Weekend A Sunday recovery.

Standard rice cake (use this one)

Batch count from the ride-length calculator. Alternate to cinnamon sweet every other weekend if you want variety — same batch maths. Other variants below are optional.

Optional variations (~27 g carbs / piece)

Cook sticky sushi rice, fold toppings, press ~2 cm thick, cool 24 h in the fridge, cut into squares, foil-wrap. Rice cakes need a full day to set — make Friday for Saturday, Thursday for Sunday.

Bacon & Egg Rice Cakes
~10 pieces~27g C · ~8g P
Full batch
  • 200 g sushi rice · 350 ml water · 1 tbsp brown sugar · ¼ tsp salt
  • 4 oz smoked streaky bacon, crisped · 2 eggs, scrambled
  • 1 tbsp liquid aminos · 1 tbsp parmesan (optional)
Half batch (5 pieces)
  1. Use half of everything above — ideal for solo weekends.
  2. Fold bacon, eggs, aminos and parmesan through warm sticky rice; press, cool, cut, wrap.
  3. Savoury anchor for hours 1–3 on long rides. Shelf life 3–4 days.

Team Sky Cinnamon Rice Cakes
~20 pieces~27g C
Quarter batch (~5 pieces) — recommended solo size
  • 125 g rice · 200 ml water · 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • ½ tsp cinnamon · 2¼ tsp coconut sugar
  • 75 g cream cheese · 25 ml agave
Method

Classic Ride Flapjack
~12 bars~32g C · ~4g P30 min
Ingredients
  • 250 g rolled oats
  • 125 g butter · 125 g golden syrup · 75 g brown sugar
  • 100 g mixed dried fruit
  • Pinch salt
Method
  1. Heat oven to 180°C. Melt butter, syrup and sugar; stir in oats, fruit and salt.
  2. Press into a lined tin (~2 cm). Bake 20–25 min until golden.
  3. Cool completely, cut into 12 bars, wrap individually. Keeps 1–2 weeks airtight; freezes well.
  4. Most energy-dense pocket fuel — bonk insurance on 4–6 h days.
Energy balls: use the Date & Oat Energy Balls in section 15 below (~15 g C each — eat two together). Batch fortnightly with the rice cakes.
Carbs during exercise — race-day fuel you can make at home

11On-Bike & During-Exercise Fuel

Summer weekends: solids + electrolyte bottles only — recipes in section 14b. Winter / event prep: maltodextrin drinks below for charity-ride and Haute Route gut training. Start fuelling in the first 15 minutes of any ride over ~75 minutes. Protocol: Ride Fuelling guide.

Fuelling Primer — how much, how often
Read first
Targets
  • Summer weekends: all carbs from rice cakes, flapjack and balls; bottles = electrolyte only
  • Carbs: ~45–60 g/hr for rides 75 min–2 h; ~60–70 g/hr for 2–4 h; 75–90 g/hr beyond 4 h and on event day
  • Fluid: 500–750 ml/hr electrolyte (600–800 ml/hr in heat)
  • Sodium: ~400–700 mg/hr from electrolyte bottles
  • Event prep: above ~60 g/hr optionally add 1:0.8 maltodextrin:fructose drinks
How to apply it
  1. Start within 15 min, then take solids every 20–30 min — set a timer on long rides.
  2. Alternate savoury and sweet rice cakes; energy balls when appetite drops in heat.
  3. Gut training: progress 60 → 70–80 → 90 g/hr via more frequent solids in summer; add carb drinks for event rehearsal if needed.
  4. Pre-mix electrolyte bottles E1/E2 Friday — see hot-weather drink below.
Energy Drink — Standard (~60 g/hr)
per 500 ml bottle~60g carbs
Ingredients
  • 40 g maltodextrin
  • 20 g fructose
  • ¼ tsp salt (~575 mg sodium)
  • Squash/cordial for flavour
  • 500 ml water
Method
  1. Add everything to the bottle and shake hard until dissolved.
  2. One bottle per hour ≈ 60 g carbs + sodium covered.
  3. No-scales version: 3 tbsp white sugar + a pinch of salt + squash per 500 ml is a rough 60 g equivalent (single-source, fine up to ~60 g/hr).
Energy Drink — High-Carb Dual Source (~90 g/hr)
per 500 ml bottle~90g carbs
Ingredients
  • 55 g maltodextrin
  • 35 g fructose
  • ¼ tsp salt (~575 mg sodium)
  • Pinch magnesium chloride or Epsom salt (muscle function over 50)
  • Squash + a squeeze of lemon
  • 500 ml water
Method & use
  1. Shake until fully dissolved — it's a denser mix, give it a minute.
  2. The ~1 : 0.8 malto:fructose ratio is what lets you push past 60 g/hr without gut trouble.
  3. For long alpine days, run this plus a rice cake or banana each hour rather than forcing all 90 g into liquid — easier on the stomach.
Tenerife & the alpine event: this is the bottle to rehearse on every long ride from week 9. Dial flavour now so it's familiar when it matters.
Hot-Weather Electrolyte Drink (low carb + potassium)
per 500 ml bottleheat / hydration
Ingredients
  • 500 ml water (or replace 200 ml with coconut water for natural electrolytes)
  • ¼ tsp salt (~575 mg sodium)
  • 1 tsp sugar or honey (helps sodium uptake)
  • Juice of ½ lemon or orange
  • Pinch of "lite" salt (half sodium, half potassium) — important over 50 and in heat
Method & use
  1. Shake to dissolve. This is a hydration bottle, not a fuel bottle.
  2. On hot days run one electrolyte bottle alongside your carb bottle, and get the carbs from solids/gel.
  3. Heat acclimation block (final 10 days before the event): useful for the indoor Z2 heat sessions too.
Night-Before Concentrate (early-start batch prep)
4 × 500 ml bottles10 min
Concentrate (store in small bottle)
  • 200 ml boiling water
  • 160 g maltodextrin
  • 80 g fructose
  • 2 g salt (~½ tsp)
  • 100 ml lemon juice or squash concentrate
Method & use
  1. Dissolve maltodextrin in warm water first (stir to paste), add fructose and salt, then lemon. Pour into a small bottle.
  2. On the morning: add 100 ml concentrate to each 500 ml bottle, top with cold water, shake hard.
  3. Each bottle ≈ 60 g carbs — perfect for 5:30 am rollouts when you don't want to measure powders at dawn.
Homemade Energy Gel (flask)
~5 × 30g servings5 min
Ingredients
  • 100 g maltodextrin
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • ~80 ml warm water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Squeeze of lemon (cuts the sweetness)
Method & use
  1. Whisk everything warm until smooth; thin with a little more water to a gel consistency.
  2. Decant into a small soft flask (refillable gel flask). ~25–30 g carbs per good swig.
  3. Chase with a mouthful of water/electrolyte — concentrated carbs need fluid to absorb well.
Savoury Rice Cakes (the long-ride staple)
~8 cakes25 min~30–40g C/cake
Ingredients
  • 250 g short-grain / sushi rice (uncooked)
  • 2 eggs, scrambled
  • 4 rashers bacon (or 100 g pancetta), chopped & crisped
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce + pinch salt
  • 30 g grated parmesan (optional)
Method
  1. Cook the rice slightly past done so it's sticky; keep it warm.
  2. Scramble the eggs and crisp the bacon (do both on the griddle).
  3. Fold eggs, bacon, soy, salt and parmesan through the warm rice.
  4. Press ~2 cm thick into a lined tray, cool, cut into ~8 squares, wrap each in foil.
  5. Real food that goes down easily after 2+ hours when sweet gels start to repel — a long-ride saviour.
Where it fits: the workhorse for rides over ~2.5 h and for the charity day / alpine stages. Two cakes ≈ one bottle's worth of carbs in solid form. Eat early, before you're hungry.
Sweet Rice Cakes (banana & nut butter)
~8 cakes25 min~35g C/cake
Ingredients
  • 250 g short-grain / sushi rice (uncooked)
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 3 tbsp peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp honey or maple
  • Pinch salt + cinnamon
Method
  1. Cook the rice sticky and warm.
  2. Fold the banana, peanut butter, honey, salt and cinnamon through it.
  3. Press, cool, cut into ~8, wrap in foil.
  4. A sweeter option for the first half of a long ride; alternate with the savoury cakes to dodge flavour fatigue.
Date & Oat Energy Balls
~12 balls10 min · no bake~18g C/ball
Ingredients
  • 200 g soft pitted dates
  • 100 g porridge oats
  • 2 tbsp honey or rice syrup
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: 1 tbsp cocoa or a scoop of whey
Method
  1. Blitz the dates to a paste (a splash of water helps).
  2. Mix in oats, honey, salt (and cocoa/whey if using) to a stiff dough.
  3. Roll into ~12 balls; fridge to firm. Pocket-sized, foil-wrapped pick-me-ups.
  4. Great for the back end of a long ride or in the jersey for a quick chew on a climb.
Don't forget: the homemade oat bars (#03) double as on-bike fuel (~28 g carbs each), and a plain banana is ~25 g — count them all toward your hourly target.
Savoury Potato Bites (Ineos-style)
~20 pieces20 min~15–20g C/piece
Ingredients
  • 6 medium white potatoes
  • 1 tsp flaky sea salt
  • Optional: dried rosemary or paprika
Method
  1. Boil potatoes until just cooked (not mushy). Cut into 3 cm chunks.
  2. Season generously with salt (and herbs if using). Cool completely.
  3. Wrap 3–4 pieces per foil packet. The salt load makes these excellent electrolyte sources in hot weather or rides over 3 h.
  4. Incredibly easy to digest at high intensities — alternate with rice cakes from hour 2 onward.
Baked Banana & Honey Bars
~12 bars30 min~25–30g C/bar
Ingredients
  • 3 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 200 g rolled oats
  • 100 g dried cranberries or raisins
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil (melted)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract + pinch salt
  • Optional: sesame or sunflower seeds
Method
  1. Mix all ingredients. Press into a lined tin (~2 cm deep).
  2. Bake 180°C for 20–25 min until golden and set. Cool completely, slice into bars.
  3. Wrap individually in foil or beeswax wrap. Holds together better than balls — easier one-handed eating on the bike.
Malt Loaf Slabs (zero prep)
emergency backup2 min~30g C/slice
What you need
  • 1 standard Soreen malt loaf
  • Optional: light scrape of unsalted butter
Method
  1. Pre-slice the night before into thick slabs (~60 g each ≈ 30 g carbs).
  2. Wrap each slice in foil. Sticky enough to survive jersey-pocket abuse.
  3. Fast-absorbed maltose — your emergency/lazy backup that also works brilliantly on long rides.
Emergency Pockets (shop-bought, zero prep)
jersey stash0 min
Keep in stock
  • Fig rolls — ~10 g carbs each, digestively gentle, individually wrapped
  • Medjool dates — ~18 g carbs each; pack in a small zip bag
  • Soreen mini malt loaf bars — pre-portioned, pocket-perfect
  • Jelly babies — ~6 g each; pro trick: 5–6 every 20 min ≈ 30 g/hr top-up
When to use
  1. Excellent complements to homemade fuel — not replacements for gut training on race-day mix.
  2. Jelly babies are ideal when legs fade late in a ride and solid food won't go down.
  3. Always count toward your hourly carb target.
Recovery Chocolate Milk after, not during
1 serving2 min
Ingredients
  • 300 ml semi-skimmed milk
  • 1–2 tsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp sugar or honey
Method
  1. Whisk cocoa + sugar with a splash of warm milk to a paste (stops lumps), top with cold milk, shake.
  2. Drink within 45 min of finishing — the carb-to-protein ratio is near-ideal for recovery, then eat the big recovery porridge (#06).
