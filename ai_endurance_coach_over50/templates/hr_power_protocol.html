Power Meter Setup & FTP Test Protocol

Don't test on day one. A baseline is only worth having if it's clean and repeatable — rush the setup and every number after it is built on sand. Three phases: dial it in, learn to trust it, then test.

The one rule that ruins baselines

The same mistake that buried you in 2012 ruins FTP tests: going out too hard. Start the test effort controlled and build — a test you blow up 8 minutes into is a junk number. Pace it like a climber, not a lunatic.

Phase 1
Setup — before the first pedal stroke

Fit the pedals

  • Charge both pedals fully first (magnetic charger clips on; LED tells you when done).
  • Grease the threads and screw in with an 8mm hex or pedal spanner. Right pedal = normal thread; left pedal = reverse thread (turns toward the back of the bike to tighten). Snug, ~35–40 Nm — firm but don't gorilla it.
  • Fit Look-compatible cleats (ships with Favero's). Match your old cleat position exactly — fore/aft, rotation, float — so your knees feel no change.

⚠ Two things that silently corrupt every number

1. Crank length. In the Favero app, set your exact crank length — it's stamped on the inside of the crank arm (likely 170 or 172.5 mm). Wrong length = wrong power, permanently. 2. Saddle height. Power pedals have a slightly different stack height than your old pedals; re-measure saddle height and match it, or you've quietly changed your fit. At 50+, protect the knees — if unsure, grab a quick bike-fit check.

App + head unit

  • Install the Favero Assioma app, pair the pedals, update firmware, and confirm/enter crank length.
  • Pair to your Garmin over ANT+ (Sensors → Add → Power). Set crank length on the Garmin too if it asks. Enable auto-zero.
  • Add a 3-second power data field to your screen (raw 1-second power is too jumpy to pace by; more on this below).

Phase 2
First 1–2 rides — bed in, don't test

Resist the urge to test immediately. Use a ride or two to confirm the data is clean and your fit is right, so the eventual baseline is trustworthy.

  • Do a manual zero-offset (calibration) before each ride. Bike level, pedals unclipped and stationary, spin the cranks backward a couple of turns, then trigger calibrate in the Favero app or on the Garmin. Takes 5 seconds.
  • Ride easy 60–90 min and just watch the numbers. Power should respond instantly and sensibly, no dropouts, no crazy spikes. Get used to the new pedals' clip-in and release tension.
  • Throw in 2–3 short efforts (30–60s harder) just to see the meter react across the range. Not a test — a sanity check.
  • Note how it feels vs the watts — start building the link between effort and number. Check your knees feel exactly as before (fit confirmation).

Why 3-second power, not 1-second

Raw power jumps around every pedal stroke and is impossible to pace by. A 3-second average smooths it enough to hold a target steadily — the field you'll live by on race climbs. Keep raw/1s off the main screen.

Phase 3
The FTP test — getting a number worth keeping

Prep (get this right or re-test)

  • Fresh legs — test the day after a rest or easy day, never after a hard block.
  • Fuelled & hydrated — normal carbs that day, a snack 1–2h before. (Don't test in a deficit-induced hole — the 2012 fuelling lesson applies.)
  • Indoors on the turbo if possible — no coasting, no traffic, no descents, controlled temperature. Far more repeatable than outdoors, which is what makes future comparisons valid.
  • Calibrate (zero-offset) immediately before. Have a fan on indoors.

Pick your test

Option A — Ramp test ← recommended for your first one

No pacing skill needed (you literally can't go out too hard), and it's over quicker. Ideal given your pacing history.

  1. Warm up 10–15 min easy with 2–3 short spin-ups.
  2. Start at ~100 W, increase +20 W every minute, hold cadence ~85–95.
  3. Keep going until you cannot hold the next step (legs fail / cadence collapses). Note your best 1-minute power.
  4. FTP ≈ 75% of that best 1-minute power.
  5. Spin easy 10 min to cool down.

Option B — 20-minute test (classic, do this one next time)

More accurate but needs pacing discipline — best saved for once you have a ballpark from the ramp.

  1. Warm up ~20 min: easy building, include 3×1 min fast (1 min easy between), then 5 min easy.
  2. One 5-min hard opener at a strong effort, then 10 min very easy.
  3. 20 min all-out, evenly paced. Aim for the highest average you can hold the whole way — first 5 min should feel almost too controlled, build slightly into the back half. Eyes on 3s power.
  4. FTP = 95% of your 20-minute average power.
  5. Cool down 10–15 min easy.

After the test

Enter the FTP in the Favero app, your Garmin, and the coach app. Then lock in your protocol — same test, same warm-up, indoors — and re-test every 4–6 weeks. The trend matters more than any single number; only same-protocol tests are comparable. Seed your ramp start sensibly next time (your current estimated FTP is ~180–210 W, so 100 W start gives plenty of runway).
