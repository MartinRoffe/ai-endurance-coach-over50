Batch Prep — the protein engine

01Batch-Roasted Chicken Thighs

The single most important prep. One tray covers six lunches (Mon/Tue/Thu rice bowls + Wed Chinese variation + Sat/Sun chicken & salad) and feeds into a griddle night. Thighs stay moist far better than breast and are cheaper per cooked gram.

Herb & Paprika Roast Thighs
Batch · Sunday ~1.45kg cooked · 6 portions 35 min ~58g P / 240g portion
Ingredients
  • 1.8 kg boneless, skinless chicken thigh fillets — ~1.5 kg for this tray, ~300 g reserved for the Chinese Wed portion (#01b)
  • 1.5 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp mixed dried herbs
  • 1 tsp garlic granules
  • ¾ tsp salt + black pepper
  • Juice of ½ lemon (optional)
Method
  1. Heat oven to 200°C fan. Line two baking trays.
  2. Toss thighs with the oil, paprika, herbs, garlic, salt and pepper until evenly coated.
  3. Spread out in a single layer, not crowded (use both trays).
  4. Roast 25–30 min until cooked through (no pink, juices run clear, 75°C+ internal).
  5. Rest 5 min, squeeze over lemon, then cool completely before portioning.
  6. Divide into 5 × ~240g herb portions — one 350 ml tub each for Mon, Tue, Thu and the two weekend (Sat/Sun) slots. Fridge Mon/Tue/Thu; freeze the two weekend portions from the off and defrost Friday night.
  7. The Wednesday Chinese portion (#01b) roasts on its own small tray at the same time — tub and label it Wed.
Food-safety: wash hands, board and knife with hot soapy water after handling raw chicken. Cut the biggest piece — inside must be white, never pink. Cook to 75°C+ internal.
Tip: The two weekend portions go straight in the freezer on Sunday — defrost Friday night for Saturday's chicken & salad. Keep a portion aside un-tubbed for a weekend griddle night if you prefer it fresh.
Soy-Glazed Chinese Thighs (Wednesday portion)
Batch · Sunday ~240g cooked · 1 portion +5 min prep · same roast ~58g P / 240g portion
Ingredients (~300g raw — from the same 1.8 kg pack)
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp runny honey
  • 2 garlic cloves, grated (or ½ tsp garlic granules)
  • 1 tsp grated ginger (or ½ tsp ginger paste)
  • Pinch white pepper
Method
  1. While the oven heats, toss the Wed thighs in the marinade. Rest 15 min — the soy and oyster sauce are the glaze; no separate finishing step.
  2. Spread on a small lined tray in a single layer alongside the main herb batch (#01).
  3. Roast at the same 200°C, 25–30 min until cooked through (75°C+ internal, juices clear).
  4. Cool completely, then portion ~240g into a 350 ml tub labelled Wed. Pair with the Wed rice tub for egg fried rice at lunch (#11b).
Why thighs: the same cut as the main batch — they stay juicy under a soy glaze and reheat well. Slices or bite-sized pieces are fine when you eat; no need to portion that way on Sunday.

02Big-Batch Basmati + 3 Variations

One smaller pot of plain basmati for the four weekday rice lunches (Mon–Thu). No weekend rice and no Friday rice (Friday is the paella pouch), so a single Sunday batch covers the whole week within its fridge life. Optionally season a tub differently for variety — same macros.

Base Batch Basmati
Batch · Sunday ~0.8kg cooked · 4 portions 25 min
Ingredients
  • 300 g basmati rice (dry)
  • 450 ml cold water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp oil or butter
Method (absorption)
  1. Rinse rice in a sieve until the water runs clear (removes surface starch).
  2. Add rice, water, salt and oil to a large pan. Bring to the boil.
  3. Cover, drop to the lowest heat, simmer 10 min — don't lift the lid.
  4. Off the heat, rest covered 10 min, then fluff with a fork.
  5. Cool quickly (spread on a tray), then portion into 4 × ~200g servings — one 350 ml tub each (carb tub for the lunch bowl #11). Eaten Mon–Thu; nothing sits past day 4.
Food-safety note: cool cooked rice within 1 hour and refrigerate promptly; reheat once, until piping hot. Don't leave it sitting at room temperature.
Three Quick Seasonings
Stir through warm portions2 min each
Per ~200g portion
  • Mexican-spiced — ½ tsp cumin, ½ tsp paprika, pinch chilli, 1 tsp tomato purée, 2 tbsp sweetcorn + kidney beans
  • Golden turmeric — ¼ tsp turmeric, 2 tbsp peas, ½ tsp soft fried onion
  • Plain — a knob of butter + black pepper
Method
  1. Reheat the rice portion until piping hot.
  2. Stir the seasoning through; for Mexican, warm the purée/spices in a dry pan 30 sec first for depth.
  3. Pairs with the batch chicken for the Mon–Thu rice lunches.
Wednesday: leave the Wed rice tub plain — it becomes egg fried rice at lunch (#11b). Day-old (or two-day-old) cold rice fries better than fresh; the Wed tub is ideal.
Optional: split Mon/Tue/Thu tubs with paprika or cumin when portioning — Wed stays plain for frying.
Optional — the snack

03Protein Bars (optional home-bake)

Default: a shop-bought Nature Valley Protein bar (~200 kcal / ~10g protein) as the Tue/Thu mid-morning snack — grab-and-go, no baking. Bake the recipe below only if you prefer a home-made version.

Peanut Butter & Whey Flapjacks
Occasional bake makes 12 20 min ~10g P / bar
Ingredients
  • 250 g porridge oats
  • 4 tbsp peanut butter
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 2 scoops whey protein (~60g)
  • 1 ripe banana, mashed
  • 150 ml milk (to bind)
  • Optional: handful raisins / mixed seeds
Method
  1. Heat oven to 180°C fan. Line a ~20cm square tin.
  2. Gently warm the peanut butter and honey (10 sec microwave) so they mix easily.
  3. Stir everything together into a thick, sticky mix — add a splash more milk if dry.
  4. Press firmly into the tin (firm pressing = bars that hold together).
  5. Bake 15 min until just golden at the edges. Cool fully in the tin.
  6. Cut into 12. Airtight container 5 days, or freeze. Only an occasional bake — the default Tue/Thu snack is the shop-bought Nature Valley bar.
When to eat: Tue and Thu mid-morning only — skipped Mon/Wed/Fri (overnight oats mornings).

04Greek-Yogurt Pots

Not really cooking — but portioning the tub on Sunday is what makes the grab-and-go habit work. Does the old GetPro pot's job at a third of the cost.

Portioned Yogurt Pots
Batch · Sunday ~6 pots 5 min ~15g P / pot
Ingredients
  • ~1.5 kg Greek-style yogurt (1–2 tubs)
  • Optional toppings, kept separate: berries, banana, honey
Method
  1. Portion ~6 small lidded grab pots (~150g each) — just the Mon–Thu lunch top-ups plus Saturday post-ruck.
  2. Leave the rest in the tub for the overnight-oats jars (#05) and the Tue/Thu scotch-egg breakfasts (200g each).
  3. Leave pots plain; add fruit/honey only when you eat them (keeps them fresh).
Why a tub: 1kg of Greek yogurt is ~£1.70 and gives ~6 pots — versus six single GetPro pots at ~£6. ~6 grab pots plus what the oats jars and scotch breakfasts draw uses ~1.5 kg a week.
Breakfast — Mon / Wed / Fri

05High-Protein Overnight Oats

Every week Mon/Wed/Fri. Makes 3 × 350 ml oat jars + 3 berry pots in ~8 min hands-on. No separate AM oat bar on these days.

Overnight Oats — 350 ml Jar
Batch · Sunday3 jars + 3 berry pots~8 min~42g P / jar
Per 350 ml jar (oats mix)
  • 50 g porridge oats
  • 1 scoop whey or casein (~30g) — keep full scoop
  • 120 ml milk
  • 90 g Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 tsp honey
Method
  1. Mix oats, protein powder and chia in a 350 ml jar; stir to distribute the powder.
  2. Add milk, yogurt and honey; stir until no dry patches remain (thick but pourable — correct).
  3. Portion 100 g berries (frozen fine) into a separate small pot or bag per breakfast — not in the oat jar.
  4. Seal both; refrigerate. Eat cold — stir berries through at the desk. Keeps 4–5 days.
Why this split: berries out of the jar frees volume so you keep the full whey scoop and 90 g yogurt (~42 g protein total with berries). If it's too thick after soaking, add 1–2 tbsp milk when you eat.
Flavour variety: EF Pro, Strawberries 'n' Cream, Classic, Quick Banoffee and more — full overnight oats flavour guide →
Recovery week — Tue / Thu only

06Egg & Veg Muffins

Recovery week (week 4) Tue/Thu breakfast instead of scotch eggs. One small tray of 6 — eat 2 per breakfast (Tue + Thu), with a couple spare.

Egg & Vegetable Muffins
Batch · Sunday6 muffins~15 min + 20–25 min bake~28g P / 2 muffins
Ingredients
  • 5 eggs
  • 100 g smoked bacon or ham, diced
  • 50 g cooked potato, diced
  • ½ red pepper, diced
  • 1 handful spinach
  • Salt, pepper, paprika; 1 tsp oil for the pan
Method
  1. Preheat oven to 180°C fan. Line 6 holes of a muffin tray.
  2. Cook bacon in oil 4–5 min; cool slightly. Dice pepper.
  3. Whisk all 5 eggs; fold in bacon, potato, pepper and spinach.
  4. Fill cases three-quarters full. Bake 20–25 min until set and golden.
  5. Cool; store in fridge 4 days. Reheat 40 sec in microwave or eat cold.
Safety check: centre must be fully set — knife inserted should come out clean, not wet.
Breakfast — Tue / Thu

07Baked Scotch Eggs + Yogurt

Every build-week Tue/Thu breakfast: 1 scotch egg + 200g Greek yogurt (~32g protein). Make just 2 on Sunday — they only keep 4 days, so a bigger batch spoils.

Lean Baked Scotch Eggs
Batch · Sunday2 eggs~30 min~32g P with yogurt
Ingredients
  • 2 eggs, hard-boiled and peeled
  • ~200 g lean turkey or chicken mince
  • 1 tsp mustard powder, salt, pepper, mixed herbs
  • Optional: light oat coating instead of breadcrumbs
  • + 200 g Greek yogurt per breakfast
Method
  1. Hard-boil eggs (9 min), cool in cold water, peel.
  2. Season mince; wrap each egg in 80–100g mince, seal completely.
  3. Optional light oat dusting on the outside.
  4. Bake on a lined tray at 200°C for 25–30 min until meat is cooked through.
  5. Cool; fridge 4 days. Each breakfast: 1 scotch egg + 200g yogurt. (Mince is usually a 500g pack — use the rest in a griddle dinner.)
Weekend & hot fallback

09Hot Porridge (3 min)

Saturday post-ruck reference. Weekday breakfasts are overnight oats (Mon/Wed/Fri) and scotch eggs (Tue/Thu).

Porridge + Greek Yogurt + Whey
1 serving3 min~35g P · 330 kcal
Ingredients
  • 50 g porridge oats
  • 250 ml milk or water
  • ½ scoop whey protein
  • 1 Greek-yogurt pot (~150g)
  • Banana or a handful of berries
Method
  1. Hot: microwave oats + milk 2–3 min, stir, let cool 1 min, then stir in the whey (add it off the heat so it stays smooth).
  2. Overnight: mix oats + milk + whey in a jar, fridge overnight, eat cold.
  3. Top with the yogurt pot and fruit.
Smooth whey: always stir whey into warm, not boiling porridge or it can clump.
Recovery — Saturday post-ruck & Sunday post-ride

10Big Recovery Porridge

The larger weekend recovery plate, eaten within 45 min of finishing. Chocolate milk first, then build the plate. ~55g protein.

The Recovery Stack
1 serving12 min~55g P · ~850 kcal
Ingredients
  • 80 g porridge oats + milk
  • 1 scoop whey
  • 2 eggs (scrambled)
  • 1 banana
  • 1 slice toast + peanut butter
  • 300 ml chocolate milk (see #11)
Method
  1. Straight off the bike: drink the chocolate milk first — fast carbs + protein.
  2. Microwave the 80g porridge; stir in the whey scoop once it's warm not hot.
  3. Scramble the 2 eggs; toast and spread the PB.
  4. Eat the lot with the banana within ~45 min of finishing.
Recovery-week version: drop to 60g oats and skip the PB toast — protein holds, carbs come down.
Lunch — Mon / Tue / Thu

11Chicken & Rice Bowl

The default weekday lunch Mon, Tue and Thu. Pure assembly from your Sunday batches — under two minutes. Portion into two 350 ml tubs (rice + chicken); yogurt stays separate. Wednesday is the Chinese variation (#11b). ~58–73g protein with the yogurt top-up.

Batch Bowl — Two-Tub Lunch
2 × 350 ml + yogurt2 min~58–73g P
Per lunch (Sunday pre-portion or morning grab)
  • Tub A (carb): ~200 g batch rice (#02), seasoned + handful salad or frozen veg
  • Tub B (protein): ~240 g batch-roasted chicken (#01)
  • 1 Greek-yogurt pot alongside (Mon–Thu)
Method
  1. On Sunday: fill paired 350 ml tubs from your batch rice and chicken stacks (#01/#02).
  2. At lunch: microwave both tubs until piping hot; eat together on one plate or straight from the tubs.
  3. Mon, Tue and Thu only. Wednesday is Chinese chicken + egg fried rice (#11b). Friday is the paella pouch (#13); the weekend is chicken + salad.
Recovery week: use 150 g chicken in tub B — same rice tub; macros match the lighter lunch day.
Lunch — Wednesday

11bChinese Chicken + Egg Fried Rice

Wednesday's weekly variation — still built from Sunday batch prep. Fry the rice Tuesday night at home (no hob at the office — microwave only). The soy-glazed thighs (#01b) reheat from the fridge; pair with egg fried rice made from your cold batch rice (the Wed tub is two days old by Tuesday — ideal). Same protein target as the standard bowl; the eggs add a little extra. Ideal fuel before the afternoon ride.

Egg Fried Rice + Chinese Chicken
Wed tub A + tub B + yogurt Fry Tue night · eat Wed ~5 min Tue · 2 min Wed ~70–85g P
Tuesday night fry (from Sunday prep)
  • ~200 g cold batch rice — Wed tub A (#02), straight from the fridge
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 spring onions, sliced (whites and greens kept separate)
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (veg or rapeseed)
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Optional: small handful frozen peas
Wednesday lunch (office)
  • 1 × 350 ml tub egg fried rice (from Tuesday night)
  • Tub B: ~240 g soy-glazed Chinese chicken (#01b)
  • 1 Greek-yogurt pot alongside
Tuesday night — fry at home (~5 min)
  1. Heat a wok or large frying pan on high. Add the oil, swirl to coat.
  2. Pour in the beaten eggs; scramble 20 sec until just set, then push to one side.
  3. Add the cold rice — break up any clumps with the back of a spoon. Stir-fry 2 min until the grains separate and pick up a little colour.
  4. Soy + sesame over the rice; toss through peas if using. Fold the eggs back in.
  5. White parts of the spring onion for 30 sec; greens off the heat.
  6. Cool quickly (spread on a plate), then pack into the Wed rice tub and refrigerate. Stack with the Chinese chicken tub for Wednesday's grab.
Wednesday lunch — microwave only (~2 min)
  1. Grab the fried-rice tub, Chinese chicken tub and yogurt pot.
  2. Microwave the rice tub until piping hot — stir halfway; splash 1 tbsp water first if it looks dry. Same for the chicken tub.
  3. Eat together on one plate with the yogurt.
Day-old rice: cold, slightly dried rice fries without going mushy — never fry rice straight from the cooker. The Wed tub (cooked Sunday, fried Tuesday) is exactly what you want.
Office workflow: no cooking at work needed — the wok step happens Tuesday evening while you're already in the kitchen. Wednesday is the same two-tub + yogurt grab as Mon/Tue/Thu, just microwave both.
Food safety: cool the fried rice within 1 hour on Tuesday night; reheat once on Wednesday until steaming hot throughout.
Optional swap — any weekday

12Tuna Pasta (optional)

Not in the fixed rotation — Wednesday is Chinese chicken + egg fried rice (#11b). Keep this as an optional higher-carb swap on any other weekday you fancy pasta over rice. ~53g protein with the yogurt alongside; one 350 ml tub (pasta) + yogurt pot.

Tuna & Sweetcorn Pasta
1 serving12 min~53g P · 78g C
Ingredients
  • 90–100 g dried pasta
  • 1 tin tuna in spring water, drained
  • 2 tbsp sweetcorn
  • Squeeze of lemon + drizzle olive oil
  • Black pepper; 1 Greek-yogurt pot alongside
Method
  1. Boil the pasta to pack timing; drain, keeping a splash of the water.
  2. Stir through tuna, sweetcorn, lemon, oil and the reserved water for a light sauce.
  3. Cook a double batch the night before and eat cold as a pasta salad if easier.
Cheaper tuna: buy the 4-tin multipack — works out well under £1 a tin.
Lunch — Friday

13Friday Paella Pouch + Prawns

Friday's zero-prep lunch: a ready-made microwave paella pouch with ~150 g cooked & peeled prawns stirred through. The prawns lift the low-protein pouch back toward target (~40g P with the day's whey shake). No batch rice needed Friday.

Pouch + Prawns (default)
1 serving3 min~40g P · 58g C
Ingredients
  • 1 ready-made paella pouch (microwave)
  • 150 g cooked & peeled prawns
  • Squeeze of lemon
Method
  1. Microwave the pouch to pack timing until piping hot.
  2. Stir the prawns through (already cooked — just warm them, don't rubberise).
  3. Finish with lemon. The Friday whey shake closes the protein gap.
Optional from-scratch version: if you'd rather cook it, make smoky prawn rice from a fresh ~200g portion of basmati with ½ tsp smoked paprika, a pinch of turmeric, 2 tbsp peas + diced pepper and the 150g prawns stirred through.
