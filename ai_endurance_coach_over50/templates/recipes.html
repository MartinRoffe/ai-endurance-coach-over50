Batch Prep — the protein engine

01Batch-Roasted Chicken Thighs

The single most important prep. One tray covers five lunches (Mon/Tue/Fri/Sat/Sun) and feeds into a griddle night. Thighs stay moist far better than breast and are cheaper per cooked gram.

Herb & Paprika Roast Thighs
Batch · Sunday ~1.2kg cooked · 5 portions 35 min ~58g P / 240g portion
Ingredients
  • 1.5 kg boneless, skinless chicken thigh fillets
  • 1.5 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 2 tsp mixed dried herbs
  • 1 tsp garlic granules
  • ¾ tsp salt + black pepper
  • Juice of ½ lemon (optional)
Method
  1. Heat oven to 200°C fan. Line two baking trays.
  2. Toss thighs with the oil, paprika, herbs, garlic, salt and pepper until evenly coated.
  3. Spread out in a single layer, not crowded (use both trays).
  4. Roast 25–30 min until cooked through (no pink, juices run clear, 75°C+ internal).
  5. Rest 5 min, squeeze over lemon, then cool completely before portioning.
  6. Divide into 5 × ~240g tubs. Fridge up to 4 days, or freeze portions 3–5 from the off and defrost as needed.
Tip: Cook a smaller second batch Wednesday if Fri/Sat chicken is running low, or simply roast 2 kg and freeze the back half. Keep a portion aside un-tubbed for a Friday griddle night.
Batch Prep — the carb base

02Big-Batch Basmati + 3 Variations

One pot of plain basmati becomes the base for every rice lunch. Season portions differently across the week so it never feels repetitive — same bag of rice, same macros.

Base Batch Basmati
Batch · Sunday ~1.3kg cooked · 6–7 portions 25 min
Ingredients
  • 500 g basmati rice (dry)
  • 750 ml cold water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tsp oil or butter
Method (absorption)
  1. Rinse rice in a sieve until the water runs clear (removes surface starch).
  2. Add rice, water, salt and oil to a large pan. Bring to the boil.
  3. Cover, drop to the lowest heat, simmer 10 min — don't lift the lid.
  4. Off the heat, rest covered 10 min, then fluff with a fork.
  5. Cool quickly (spread on a tray), then portion. Fridge ≤3 days, or freeze.
Food-safety note: cool cooked rice within 1 hour and refrigerate promptly; reheat once, until piping hot. Don't leave it sitting at room temperature.
Three Quick Seasonings
Stir through warm portions2 min each
Per ~200g portion
  • Mexican-spiced — ½ tsp cumin, ½ tsp paprika, pinch chilli, 1 tsp tomato purée, 2 tbsp sweetcorn + kidney beans
  • Golden turmeric — ¼ tsp turmeric, 2 tbsp peas, ½ tsp soft fried onion
  • Plain — a knob of butter + black pepper
Method
  1. Reheat the rice portion until piping hot.
  2. Stir the seasoning through; for Mexican, warm the purée/spices in a dry pan 30 sec first for depth.
  3. Pairs with the batch chicken for Mon/Tue/Fri/Sat/Sun lunches.
Weeks 2 & 3 rotate which day gets which seasoning — that's the only thing that changes between build weeks.

03Homemade Oat Bars

Replaces the shop protein bars at ~25p each. One tray is a week of AM snacks, and they double as on-bike fuel for the long ride.

Peanut Butter & Whey Flapjacks
Batch · Sunday 12 bars 20 min ~10g P / bar
Ingredients
  • 250 g porridge oats
  • 4 tbsp peanut butter
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 2 scoops whey protein (~60g)
  • 1 ripe banana, mashed
  • 150 ml milk (to bind)
  • Optional: handful raisins / mixed seeds
Method
  1. Heat oven to 180°C fan. Line a ~20cm square tin.
  2. Gently warm the peanut butter and honey (10 sec microwave) so they mix easily.
  3. Stir everything together into a thick, sticky mix — add a splash more milk if dry.
  4. Press firmly into the tin (firm pressing = bars that hold together).
  5. Bake 15 min until just golden at the edges. Cool fully in the tin.
  6. Cut into 12. Airtight container 5 days, or freeze.
On-bike use: one bar is ~28g carbs — wrap a couple for the long ride and count them toward your 60–90 g/hr.
Batch Prep — the protein top-up

04Greek-Yogurt Pots

Not really cooking — but portioning the tub on Sunday is what makes the grab-and-go habit work. Does the old GetPro pot's job at a third of the cost.

Portioned Yogurt Pots
Batch · Sunday ~12 pots 5 min ~15g P / pot
Ingredients
  • 2 × 1kg Greek-style yogurt tubs
  • Optional toppings, kept separate: berries, banana, honey, a sprinkle of the oat-bar mix
Method
  1. Spoon yogurt into ~12 small lidded pots (~150g each).
  2. Leave plain; add fruit/honey only when you eat them (keeps them fresh).
  3. Uses: breakfast top-up daily, lunch top-up Mon–Thu, Saturday post-ruck.
Why a tub: 1kg of Greek yogurt is ~£1.70 and gives ~6 pots — versus six single GetPro pots at ~£6.
Breakfast — every morning

05Everyday Porridge

The daily breakfast: ~35g protein, ~330 kcal. Make it overnight (cold) or in 3 minutes (hot) — same macros either way.

Porridge + Greek Yogurt + Whey
1 serving3 min~35g P · 330 kcal
Ingredients
  • 50 g porridge oats
  • 250 ml milk or water
  • ½ scoop whey protein
  • 1 Greek-yogurt pot (~150g)
  • Banana or a handful of berries
Method
  1. Hot: microwave oats + milk 2–3 min, stir, let cool 1 min, then stir in the whey (add it off the heat so it stays smooth).
  2. Overnight: mix oats + milk + whey in a jar, fridge overnight, eat cold.
  3. Top with the yogurt pot and fruit.
Smooth whey: always stir whey into warm, not boiling porridge or it can clump.
Recovery — Saturday post-ruck & Sunday post-ride

06Big Recovery Porridge

The larger weekend recovery plate, eaten within 45 min of finishing. Chocolate milk first, then build the plate. ~55g protein.

The Recovery Stack
1 serving12 min~55g P · ~850 kcal
Ingredients
  • 80 g porridge oats + milk
  • 1 scoop whey
  • 2 eggs (scrambled)
  • 1 banana
  • 1 slice toast + peanut butter
  • 300 ml chocolate milk (see #11)
Method
  1. Straight off the bike: drink the chocolate milk first — fast carbs + protein.
  2. Microwave the 80g porridge; stir in the whey scoop once it's warm not hot.
  3. Scramble the 2 eggs; toast and spread the PB.
  4. Eat the lot with the banana within ~45 min of finishing.
Recovery-week version: drop to 60g oats and skip the PB toast — protein holds, carbs come down.

07Chicken & Rice Bowl

Pure assembly from your Sunday batches — under two minutes. ~58g protein with the yogurt top-up on weekdays.

Batch Bowl
1 serving2 min~58–73g P
Ingredients
  • 240 g batch-roasted chicken (#01)
  • ~200 g batch rice (#02), seasoned
  • Handful of salad or frozen veg
  • 1 Greek-yogurt pot alongside (Mon–Thu)
Method
  1. Combine chicken + rice in a microwave tub; reheat until piping hot.

08Tuna Pasta

Higher-carb ride-day lunch from bulk pasta. ~53g protein with the yogurt alongside.

Tuna & Sweetcorn Pasta
1 serving12 min~53g P · 78g C
Ingredients
  • 90–100 g dried pasta
  • 1 tin tuna in spring water, drained
  • 2 tbsp sweetcorn
  • Squeeze of lemon + drizzle olive oil
  • Black pepper; 1 Greek-yogurt pot alongside
Method
  1. Boil the pasta to pack timing; drain, keeping a splash of the water.
  2. Stir through tuna, sweetcorn, lemon, oil and the reserved water for a light sauce.
  3. Cook a double batch the night before and eat cold as a pasta salad if easier.
Cheaper tuna: buy the 4-tin multipack — works out well under £1 a tin.

09Paprika Rice & Prawns

The homemade "paella" that replaced the Ben's pouch — same carb base for the evening kettlebell session. ~47g protein.

Smoky Prawn Rice
1 serving5 min~47g P · 62g C
Ingredients
  • ~200 g batch rice (#02)
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika + pinch turmeric
  • 2 tbsp peas + diced pepper
  • 150 g cooked & peeled prawns
  • Squeeze of lemon; 1 Greek-yogurt pot alongside
Method

  3. Finish with lemon. Yogurt pot alongside for the protein top-up.
Dinner & weekend — the Blackstone flat top

10Blackstone Griddle — Method + 6 Recipes

Your full griddle book, folded in and tagged for training. Each recipe serves 4 (2 adults, 2 kids) — for a hungry training day, scale up your own rice/protein portion. The "Where it fits" note on each tells you which day of the week it belongs to and how to make it work for the riding.

Griddle Primer
Read first
Set-up
  • Preheat 8–10 min; run a hotter zone and a cooler zone
  • Thin film of high-smoke-point oil (rapeseed/veg)
  • Scraper, melting dome, and a squirt bottle of water for steam
Principles
  1. Protein on the hot zone for the sear; finish on the cooler zone so it cooks through without burning.
  2. Veg, tortillas and flatbreads on the cooler zone alongside.
  3. Rest meat 3–5 min before slicing. Scrape down while the surface is still hot.
01 · Griddle Full English
Breakfast30 min · Med-HighServes 4~35g P/adult
Ingredients
  • 8 pork sausages
  • 8 back bacon rashers
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 thick bread slices
  • 4 vine tomatoes, halved
  • 200 g chestnut mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tbsp veg oil + 2 tbsp butter
  • Salt & black pepper
Method
  1. Sausages first on a medium-high zone with the oil — they need the most time, ~8 min, turning.
  2. Add bacon and tomatoes (cut-side down); melt butter on the second zone for the mushrooms.
  3. Flip bacon, push sausages to the cool zone, butter and toast the bread directly on the griddle.
  4. Drop a zone to low, crack the eggs straight on, season, cover with the dome 2–3 min for set whites/runny yolks.
  5. Everything lands together — plate and eat fast, a flat-top fry-up cools quickly.
Where it fits: a weekend treat or a post-long-ride brunch — high protein and fat, so it's recovery/rest-day food, not a pre-ride meal (the fat and fibre sit heavy before riding).
Coach tweak: to make it a proper recovery plate, add the toast (and beans, heated on the hob) for carbs, go an extra egg, and lean on the bacon over the sausage for cleaner protein. Black pudding griddles well — 2 min a side.
02 · American Pancakes & Crispy Bacon
Breakfast25 min · MediumServes 4carb-rich
Ingredients
  • Batter
  • 300 g plain flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder · 2 tbsp caster sugar · ½ tsp salt
  • 300 ml whole milk · 2 eggs
  • 3 tbsp melted butter · 1 tsp vanilla
  • To cook
  • 12 back bacon rashers
  • 1 tbsp butter · maple syrup to serve
Method
  1. Mix dry; whisk eggs into milk with vanilla + melted butter; combine — lumps are fine, don't overmix.
  2. One zone medium for pancakes, one medium-high for bacon. Butter the pancake zone (it should foam, not brown).
  3. Cook bacon 3–4 min a side until crisp; move to the cool edge.
  4. Pour digestive-sized rounds; flip once when bubbles break across the surface and edges look dry.
  5. Stack 3–4 per person with bacon and maple syrup. Serve hot.
Where it fits: a brilliant carb-load — eat the night before or ~3 h before a long ride, or turn it into a recovery breakfast. Maple syrup is fast carbs that do a real job around training.
Coach tweak (recovery stack): beat 2 scoops whey into the batter and top with a Greek-yogurt pot + sliced banana — that turns a ~10g-protein breakfast into a 40g+ recovery meal. Blueberries/banana straight onto the batter for the kids.
03 · Double Smash Burgers
Dinner15 min · Max heatServes 4~40g P/adult
Ingredients
  • 600 g beef mince, 20% fat
  • 4 brioche buns · 8 slices American cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter · salt & pepper
  • Sauce
  • 4 tbsp mayo · 2 tbsp ketchup · 1 tbsp mustard · 1 tbsp gherkin relish
  • Topping
  • Iceberg lettuce · 2 tomatoes, sliced
Method
  1. Mix the sauce; chill. Roll mince into 8 loose ~75g balls, keep cold.
  2. Toast buttered buns on medium-high; set aside.
  3. Crank to max. Place balls with space, smash flat within 5 seconds, season — then don't touch.
  4. Flip once at a dark crust, cheese on, dome to melt ~45 sec.
  5. Sauce both buns, build with lettuce/tomato, two patties per adult. Serve immediately.
Where it fits: an indulgence — best on a rest day or as a post-event reward. The 20% fat that makes it great also makes it heavy fuel mid-training-block.
Coach tweak (training-week version): use 15% mince (5% won't smash as well but works pressed thicker), a wholemeal/seeded bun, and pile the salad. Still ~40g protein, far less saturated fat.
04 · Chicken & Pepper Fajitas ★ Friday
Dinner25 min · HighServes 4~40g P/adult
Ingredients
  • 4 chicken breasts (or thighs from batch), in strips
  • 3 mixed peppers · 2 red onions, sliced
  • 3 tbsp fajita seasoning · 4 tbsp oil
  • 8 flour tortillas
  • To serve
  • Sour cream · salsa · 100 g cheddar · lime
Method
  1. Toss chicken with 2 tbsp oil + the seasoning; marinate 15 min if you can.
  2. Both zones high. Lay chicken in a single layer, leave 3–4 min for sizzle not steam.
  3. Flip chicken; on the other zone char the peppers and onions until soft with colour.
  4. Clear a space, char the tortillas 30 sec a side, wrap to keep warm.
  5. Build at the table — chicken, veg, sour cream, salsa, cheddar, lime.
Where it fits: this is your Friday griddle night — the ideal training dinner: lean protein, a pile of veg, and tortilla carbs you can dial up or down. Batch chicken thighs slot straight in.
Coach tweak: on a weeknight go easy on the cheese/sour cream and add an extra tortilla for carbs; full-fat the toppings at the weekend. DIY seasoning: 2 tsp smoked paprika, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp garlic powder, ½ tsp chilli, ½ tsp oregano, ½ tsp salt.
05 · Chicken & Chorizo Paella ★ Saturday pre-ride
Dinner45 min · Med→LowServes 4high carb
Ingredients
  • 4 bone-in chicken thighs · 150 g cooking chorizo
  • 300 g paella rice (Bomba/Calasparra)
  • 900 ml hot chicken stock · 1 pinch saffron
  • 1 onion · 4 garlic cloves · 2 tomatoes, grated
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika · 100 g peas
  • 3 tbsp olive oil · lemon · parsley
Method
  1. Steep saffron in the hot stock ~10 min until deep gold.
  2. Brown seasoned thighs skin-down ~6 min; add chorizo until the fat runs; move to the edges.
  3. Sofrito in the centre: onion 3 min, garlic + paprika 1 min, grated tomato until darkened.
  4. Stir rice through to coat, toast 1–2 min, spread everything out with the chicken/chorizo.
  5. Pour the saffron stock evenly — now do not stir. Medium-low ~18–20 min; listen for the crackle of the socarrat.
  6. Peas in for the last 3 min; rest 5 min under the hood. Parsley + lemon, scrape up the crust.
Where it fits: this is your carb-rich Saturday dinner — the real pre-ride fuel for Sunday's long ride. Big bowl of rice = a topped-up glycogen tank before an early start.
Coach tweak: go generous on the rice and easy on the chorizo to keep the fat down the night before a big effort; add prawns in the last 4 min for extra lean protein. Don't swap the Bomba rice — basmati/risotto rice won't behave.
06 · Chinese-Style Beef Stir Fry ★ Sunday recovery
Dinner20 min · Max heatServes 4~38g P/adult
Ingredients
  • 500 g sirloin/rump, thin sliced + 2 tbsp soy, 1 tbsp cornflour, 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Sauce
  • 3 tbsp oyster · 2 tbsp soy · 1 tbsp rice vinegar · 1 tsp sugar · 1 tsp cornflour + 2 tbsp water
  • Stir fry
  • 200 g tenderstem · 2 pak choi · 150 g mushrooms
  • 4 garlic · 30 g ginger · 4 spring onions · 3 tbsp oil
  • Steamed rice + sesame seeds to serve
Method
  1. Velvet the beef in soy/cornflour/sesame, 15 min. Mix the sauce.
  2. Max heat + oil. Sear beef in a single layer, 90 sec, in 2–3 batches; move to the edge.
  3. Garlic + ginger 30 sec until fragrant.
  4. Broccoli + mushrooms first; pak choi stems, then leaves; a splash of water to steam through.
  5. Beef back in, sauce over, toss ~30 sec until glossy. Spring onions, sesame, over rice.
Where it fits: an excellent Sunday post-long-ride recovery dinner — lean protein, a load of vegetables, and rice to refill glycogen. Quick enough after a big day out.
Coach tweak: pile the rice high for recovery, and chicken-thigh strips work identically if you'd rather go leaner than beef. Velveting (the cornflour coat) is what keeps the meat tender — don't skip it.
Carbs during exercise — race-day fuel you can make at home

11On-Bike & During-Exercise Fuel

Shop gels and drink mixes are expensive and you go through a lot of them. These DIY versions cost pennies, taste better, and — critically — let you train your gut on exactly what you'll race on. Start fuelling in the first 15 minutes of any ride over ~75 minutes and keep it steady. For when to eat what on early weekend long rides vs evening sessions, see the dedicated Ride Fuelling guide.

Fuelling Primer — how much, how often
Read first
Targets
  • Carbs: ~60 g/hr for rides 1–2.5 h; 75–90 g/hr beyond 2.5 h and on event day
  • Fluid: 500–750 ml/hr (more in heat)
  • Sodium: ~300–700 mg/hr; more if you're a salty/heavy sweater
  • Ratio: above ~60 g/hr use ~1 : 0.8 glucose : fructose to raise the absorption ceiling
How to apply it
  1. Start within 15 min, then take something every 20–30 min — don't wait until you feel empty.
  2. Mix sources: some drink, some solid (rice cake/bar), the odd gel for a hard effort. Variety beats flavour fatigue over 4+ hours.
  3. Gut training: rehearse the full race-day rate on your long rides now (weeks 9+ to 90 g/hr) — the gut adapts. Nothing new on event day.
  4. In heat, lean harder on the electrolyte drink and keep carbs in solids/gel.
Energy Drink — Standard (~60 g/hr)
per 500 ml bottle~60g carbs
Ingredients
  • 40 g maltodextrin
  • 20 g fructose
  • ¼ tsp salt (~575 mg sodium)
  • Squash/cordial for flavour
  • 500 ml water
Method
  1. Add everything to the bottle and shake hard until dissolved.
  2. One bottle per hour ≈ 60 g carbs + sodium covered.
  3. No-scales version: 3 tbsp white sugar + a pinch of salt + squash per 500 ml is a rough 60 g equivalent (single-source, fine up to ~60 g/hr).
Energy Drink — High-Carb Dual Source (~90 g/hr)
per 500 ml bottle~90g carbs
Ingredients
  • 55 g maltodextrin
  • 35 g fructose
  • ¼ tsp salt (~575 mg sodium)
  • Squash + a squeeze of lemon
  • 500 ml water
Method & use
  1. Shake until fully dissolved — it's a denser mix, give it a minute.
  2. The ~1 : 0.8 malto:fructose ratio is what lets you push past 60 g/hr without gut trouble.
  3. For long alpine days, run this plus a rice cake or banana each hour rather than forcing all 90 g into liquid — easier on the stomach.
Tenerife & the alpine event: this is the bottle to rehearse on every long ride from week 9. Dial flavour now so it's familiar when it matters.
Hot-Weather Electrolyte Drink (low carb)
per 500 ml bottleheat / hydration
Ingredients
  • 500 ml water
  • ¼ tsp salt (~575 mg sodium)
  • 1 tsp sugar or honey (helps sodium uptake)
  • Juice of ½ lemon or orange
  • Pinch of "lite" salt (adds potassium) if you have it
Method & use
  1. Shake to dissolve. This is a hydration bottle, not a fuel bottle.
  2. On hot days run one electrolyte bottle alongside your carb bottle, and get the carbs from solids/gel.
  3. Heat acclimation block (final 10 days before the event): useful for the indoor Z2 heat sessions too.
Homemade Energy Gel (flask)
~5 × 30g servings5 min
Ingredients
  • 100 g maltodextrin
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • ~80 ml warm water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Squeeze of lemon (cuts the sweetness)
Method & use
  1. Whisk everything warm until smooth; thin with a little more water to a gel consistency.
  2. Decant into a small soft flask (refillable gel flask). ~25–30 g carbs per good swig.
  3. Chase with a mouthful of water/electrolyte — concentrated carbs need fluid to absorb well.
Savoury Rice Cakes (the long-ride staple)
~8 cakes25 min~30–40g C/cake
Ingredients
  • 250 g short-grain / sushi rice (uncooked)
  • 2 eggs, scrambled
  • 4 rashers bacon (or 100 g pancetta), chopped & crisped
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce + pinch salt
  • 30 g grated parmesan (optional)
Method
  1. Cook the rice slightly past done so it's sticky; keep it warm.
  2. Scramble the eggs and crisp the bacon (do both on the griddle).
  3. Fold eggs, bacon, soy, salt and parmesan through the warm rice.
  4. Press ~2 cm thick into a lined tray, cool, cut into ~8 squares, wrap each in foil.
  5. Real food that goes down easily after 2+ hours when sweet gels start to repel — a long-ride saviour.
Where it fits: the workhorse for rides over ~2.5 h and for the charity day / alpine stages. Two cakes ≈ one bottle's worth of carbs in solid form. Eat early, before you're hungry.
Sweet Rice Cakes (banana & nut butter)
~8 cakes25 min~35g C/cake
Ingredients
  • 250 g short-grain / sushi rice (uncooked)
  • 2 ripe bananas, mashed
  • 3 tbsp peanut butter
  • 2 tbsp honey or maple
  • Pinch salt + cinnamon
Method
  1. Cook the rice sticky and warm.
  2. Fold the banana, peanut butter, honey, salt and cinnamon through it.
  3. Press, cool, cut into ~8, wrap in foil.
  4. A sweeter option for the first half of a long ride; alternate with the savoury cakes to dodge flavour fatigue.
Date & Oat Energy Balls
~12 balls10 min · no bake~18g C/ball
Ingredients
  • 200 g soft pitted dates
  • 100 g porridge oats
  • 2 tbsp honey or rice syrup
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional: 1 tbsp cocoa or a scoop of whey
Method
  1. Blitz the dates to a paste (a splash of water helps).
  2. Mix in oats, honey, salt (and cocoa/whey if using) to a stiff dough.
  3. Roll into ~12 balls; fridge to firm. Pocket-sized, foil-wrapped pick-me-ups.
  4. Great for the back end of a long ride or in the jersey for a quick chew on a climb.
Don't forget: the homemade oat bars (#03) double as on-bike fuel (~28 g carbs each), and a plain banana is ~25 g — count them all toward your hourly target.
Recovery Chocolate Milk after, not during
1 serving2 min
Ingredients
  • 300 ml semi-skimmed milk
  • 1–2 tsp cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp sugar or honey
Method
  1. Whisk cocoa + sugar with a splash of warm milk to a paste (stops lumps), top with cold milk, shake.
  2. Drink within 45 min of finishing — the carb-to-protein ratio is near-ideal for recovery, then eat the big recovery porridge (#06).
