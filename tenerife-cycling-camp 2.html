Camp Philosophy
Built Like a Pro Block

You currently ride 160km/week. This camp takes you to approximately 3× your weekly volume in a periodised structure: hard days are genuinely hard, easy days protect your legs, and coffee rides keep the joy in cycling. The week follows a classic 3:1 load-to-recovery ratio. Every ride starts and finishes at Los Gigantes harbour.

Hard · Big Climb
Teide from the West — TF-38 Ascent
90km
Distance
2,100m

▶
☕ Coffee at Bar Parada, Chío — legendary cycling café

The signature route from Los Gigantes. Head north through Tamaimo and Santiago del Teide, then follow the TF-38 all the way to the Teide high point at 2,078m. This is 36.6km of climbing gaining 2,068m at 5.6% average — one of the longest sustained climbs in Europe. Stop at Bar Parada in Chío (the pro peloton's favourite stop) before the final push. Descend back to Los Gigantes via the same route. Dress in layers — it's warm at the bottom and cold at the top.

Route

Los Gigantes → Tamaimo → Santiago del Teide → Chío → TF-38 → Teide high point → descend same route

The Climb

36.6km total / 2,068m gain / 5.6% avg. Quiet road, good surface. Very little traffic.

Effort

Climb at Zone 3–4. Find a sustainable rhythm early — the bulk of climbing is in the first 37km. Last 5km gets tough.

Gear

Gilet or jacket for descent mandatory. Arm warmers. Sunscreen for the crater. At least 3 bottles or plan to refill at Chío.

Effort

Notes

Start early (7–7:30am) to beat heat and tourist traffic. The Masca descent is technical — brake early, watch for gravel.

Pro Camp Nutrition
Eat Like You Mean It

At this volume, nutrition is training. Under-eating is the most common mistake at cycling camps. The targets below are for hard days — adjust down by 15–20% on easy and rest days.

Hard Day Calories

3,200–3,800

kcal total. More for 100km+ days with big elevation.

Carbohydrate Target

8–10g/kg

Body weight per day. Carbs are your fuel — don't fear them.

Protein Target

1.8–2.2g/kg

Per day for muscle repair. Spread across all meals.

On-Bike Fuelling

60–90g carbs per hour while riding. Gels, bars, rice cakes, bananas. Start at 30 mins in — don't wait.

Hydration

500–750ml per hour on the bike. Add electrolyte tabs — you're sweating in Tenerife's heat even in cool months. 2–3L total on big days.

Recovery Window

Within 30 mins of finishing: 60–80g carbs + 20–30g protein. Chocolate milk, rice + eggs, or recovery shake work perfectly.

Daily Meal Structure
Hard Day Template
6:00 AM — Pre-Ride
Carb-Loading Breakfast

Large bowl of oats or porridge with banana + honey. 2 slices toast with jam. Strong espresso. Small glass of juice. Eat 90 mins before riding. Avoid fibre-heavy or fatty foods.

On Bike
Continuous Fuelling

Energy gels or bars every 30–45 mins from the 30-min mark. Alternate with real food: banana halves, rice cakes, Canarian papas arrugadas (small salty potatoes — the local secret). Aim for 60–90g carbs/hr. Water + electrolytes every 15 mins.

Coffee Stop
Mid-Ride Café

Cortado or café con leche + a tostada (toast with tomato and olive oil) or a small bocadillo. The Canarian Barraquito (espresso + condensed milk + Licor 43) is a local speciality — great sugar hit but not on hard climbing days. A banana or fruit too.

Within 30 Mins
Recovery Meal — Golden Window

Don't skip this. Chocolate milk + banana is ideal if you can't face a full meal immediately. Or: rice with grilled chicken or eggs. This triggers muscle protein synthesis while glycogen stores are depleted and receptive.

Lunch
Main Recovery Meal

Large carb base: pasta, rice, or potatoes. Lean protein: chicken, fish, or eggs. Plenty of vegetables. Canarian options: Ropa Vieja (chickpea stew with shredded meat), fresh grilled fish at the harbour. Avoid heavy fried food — it sits badly before the next day's ride.

Afternoon Snack
Bridge Snack (if needed)

Greek yoghurt + honey. Or a handful of nuts + piece of fruit. Don't go to dinner starving — you'll overeat the wrong things.

Dinner
Replenishment Dinner

Another carb-forward meal to top up glycogen for tomorrow. Pasta, risotto, rice dishes. Protein from fish or meat. A glass of wine is fine — but not on the night before a hard day. Good-quality sleep matters more than any single meal.

Before Bed
Casein Protein Hit

Cottage cheese, Greek yoghurt, or a glass of warm milk. Casein digests slowly overnight and extends muscle repair while you sleep. Small portion — not a full meal.

Easy Day & Rest Day Adjustment

On easy days, reduce overall calorie intake by ~15–20% and cut back on simple carbs. Prioritise protein and vegetables. On the full rest day, eat intuitively — your hunger will drop naturally. Don't under-eat protein on rest days: that's when repair happens. Avoid the temptation to eat a huge dinner the night before a hard day — pre-loading works better spread across the whole day before.

Camp Intelligence
Pro Camp Tips
🌡️

Dress in Layers

Los Gigantes is warm at sea level. Teide at 2,000m+ can be near-freezing. Always carry a gilet and arm warmers in your back pocket on any Teide day. The descent is brutally cold.

🌅

Start Early

Aim to leave by 7–7:30am on all hard days. The roads are quieter, it's cooler for the climbs, and you'll be back before the afternoon heat hits. Teide days especially — don't start after 8am.

💧

Water Sources

Bar Parada in Chío will refill your bottles. Santiago del Teide has a fountain. The Teide Visitor Centre at the summit has water and a café. Plan your refills and carry more than you think you need.

😴

Sleep Is Training

Aim for 8–9 hours per night. This is where your body adapts. Avoid late dinners, limit alcohol to rest days, and keep screens off an hour before bed. Pro teams are in bed by 10pm.

🚲

Gearing

If hiring a bike, ask for a 34T or 32T compact chainring as minimum. Teide from Los Gigantes is relentless. Spinning a lower gear is smarter than grinding and burning out your knees on a 36km climb.

📍

Respect the Roads

Masca descent and northern mountain roads can have loose gravel especially after rain. The TF-38 to Teide has some patchy surface in places. Brake earlier than you think, especially when cold on descents.
